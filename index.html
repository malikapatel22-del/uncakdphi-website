intention and care.

The UNC-Chapel Hill Associate Chapter of alpha Kappa Delta Phi was founded by the 7 sisters of the charter class Carol Chen, Joyce Chu, Jenny Hsieh, Chetali Kuber, Trinh Le, Tina Wu, and Wendy Yang who believed that a sorority could be a space for authentic connection, meaningful impact, and cultural pride.

The Process

How to join

01

Interest Form

Fill out our interest form to let us know you're interested in learning more about our sorority. This is not a mandatory step, however it will help us get to know you better.

02

Rush Events

Participate in a series of activities designed to help us get to know each other authentically.

03

Interest Meeting

Join 1 out of 2 interest meetings to get to know our sisters and learn more about our sorority. Each semester will have one in person and one virtual.

04

Interview

Participate in an interview to discuss your interest in joining and we will answer any questions you may have. Attendance to 2 recruitment events and 1 interest meeting is required to be eligible for an interview.

FAQ

Your questions, answered

Hazing is any activity expected of someone joining a group that humiliates, degrades, abuses, or endangers them, regardless of the person's willingness to participate. We have a strict anti-hazing policy and do not condone or tolerate any form of hazing in our chapter. Our recruitment process is designed to be inclusive, respectful, and focused on building genuine connections.
Not at all. alpha Kappa Delta Phi is an Asian-interest sorority, which means we celebrate and center Asian cultures and identities, but membership is open to women of all backgrounds who share our values and vision.
Your first semester of membership will be the same time commitment as a 3 credit hour class. After that, sisters typically commit to whatever they are able to balance alongside their academics and other commitments. We have sisters who are very involved and others who take a lighter approach, both are valid and supported here.
Dues vary by semester and cover chapter operations, national fees, and events. We are committed to making membership accessible and have a payment plan process available. Reach out to us directly for current amounts.
We are a chapter that centers cultural identity alongside traditional sorority values. Our community is intentionally close-knit, academically supportive, and deeply service-oriented. Sisters often say the relationships they build here are unlike any other they've found at UNC.
We hold formal recruitment each semester. Follow our Instagram @uncakdphi for exact dates and fill out our interest form below to speak one-on-one with one of our sisters.

Our Cause

Service is not just what we do, it's who we are.

Our organization's purpose lies in supporting breast cancer awareness initiatives. From hosting educational events to fundraising for research, we are dedicated to making a positive impact in the fight against breast cancer.

We also focus on other causes close to our community: Hayley’s Gift, Swab The World Stem Cell Drive, and Violence Awareness.
